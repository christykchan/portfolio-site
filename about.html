How did I switch to UI/UX? (a little story about me...)

I got a job in a bank after I graduated from school because I couldn't figure out what to do for my future. Until one day, my manager said to me, "Christy, you are always good with the computer; why aren't you working in that field?" What she said to me was obvious in a sense, but it's something that I've never thought about, I thought it was just a hobby.

I started looking at jobs in the tech field and hoping there was something out there for me, and I found UI/UX. The moment I figured out what UI/UX is, I immediately thought to myself, why didn't I know about this? This should've been my major! I would love to do this for my future! It fits! That was one of the most exciting moments of my life. As I learned more and more about UI/UX, the more motivated I got and wanted to start doing more. Today, I finished both my UI and UX course. I'm also taking a short course for frontend development so I can communicate better with different teams and understand the technological constraints.
